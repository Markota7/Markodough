Before You Get Started
⚠ Plan ahead — this is not a same-day dough

Your poolish needs to be made 12–16 hours before you mix your dough and must sit at room temperature the entire time — never refrigerate it. After mixing, the dough cold ferments in the fridge for 48–72 hours. Total elapsed time from start to bake: up to 4 days.

⚖ A kitchen scale is essential

All measurements in this recipe are by weight. Volume measurements (cups, tablespoons) are not accurate enough for dough — especially for small ingredients like yeast and diastatic malt powder where even a small difference changes the result. A basic digital kitchen scale costs under $15 and is the single biggest upgrade a home baker can make. We strongly recommend one before proceeding.

Sauce
✓ Make your sauce before shaping

Your sauce only takes a few minutes. Making it first gives the flavors time to meld while you shape your dough.

Salt and red pepper flakes to taste
Method
1
Crush the tomatoes

Open your can of San Marzano tomatoes. Pour off about half the liquid — reserve it in case the sauce feels too thick. Using clean hands, crush the tomatoes directly into a bowl one at a time. Squeeze each through your fingers until broken down to a rough, chunky consistency. Do not over-crush — some texture is ideal for NY style. If you prefer a smoother sauce, use an immersion blender for a few short pulses instead.

2
Season and combine

Add the minced garlic, olive oil, and oregano. Stir to combine. Taste and season with salt. Add basil. If you like heat, add red pepper flakes now.

3
Set aside — do not cook

Leave your sauce at room temperature while you shape your dough. Do not cook the sauce — it will cook on the pizza in the oven.

⚠ Go light on the sauce

A light hand is key for NY style. You want to see the dough through a thin layer of sauce — not cover it completely. Too much sauce makes the center soggy and prevents the undercarriage from crisping properly.

✓ Grate your own mozzarella

Pre-shredded mozzarella contains anti-caking agents that prevent proper melting and produce a greasy, uneven result. A box grater on the large holes works well. Grate cold — it shreds more cleanly.

Pecorino Romano or Parmesan optional — to taste, applied before and after bake
